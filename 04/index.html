Are organized collections of data. They help keep data easy to find. They are used to store, organize, and manage large volumes of data. The basic concepts include fields, records (lines or rows), and columns.

A field represents a specific characteristic of an institution. For example, in a database of employees, fields could include "Name," "Employee ID," "Salary," and "Department."

A record, also known as a row or line, is a collection of fields that represent a single institution. Using the employee database example, a record would contain all the information about an employee, such as their name, employee ID, salary, and department.

A column represents a specific type of information across all records. In the employee database example, "Name," "Employee ID," "Salary," and "Department" would be columns.

Consider a simple table representing employee information:

Employee ID Name Salary Department
001 John Peterson 50000 HR
002 Jane Smith 60000 IT
003 Bob Johnson 55000 Finance

In this example:

  • "Employee ID," "Name," "Salary," and "Department" are columns.
  • Each row represents a unique employee, and together, they form records.
  • For instance, the record for John Peterson is (001, John Peterson, 50000, HR).

In business activities, databases play a crucial role.

  • They organize information in tables, making it easy to locate and manage;
  • Businesses can easily get specific information;
  • Databases make sure information is correct;
  • Databases offer user authentication and access control, to protect business information;
  • Businesses can share information using databases;
  • Databases support transactions, allowing multiple operations to be grouped together.

Data processing

In the previous studies, you learned what the basic concepts of databases are. However, in this section, you will learn about data processing and its relation to databases.

A database helps data processing by storing and organizing it in a structured form. It's la well-organized storage space that makes it easy to manage and find information, especially when there are a lot of data.

Find the definition of these terms and explain them to the big group:

Data processing refers to the conversion of raw data into information through a series of activities. Look at the infographic below to understand more about these steps and their definition and processes.

Click or tap to view content.

Data Collection

Definition: Collecting raw data from various sources, which include surveys, databases, forms, or other data input methods.

Process: Data is collected in its raw form.

Data Preparation

Definition: Cleaning and organizing raw data to ensure consistency, and completeness.

Process: This step involves managing erroneous data and converting data into a consistent structure.

Data Input

Definition: Entering prepared data into the computer system.

Process: Data is input into databases, spreadsheets, or other software applications that facilitate processing.

Data Processing

Definition: Performing operations on the input data to convert it into information.

Process: It involves computational tasks, such as filtering and performing calculations on the data. Algorithms and programming scripts are used for more processing.

Data Storage

Definition: Storing processed data for future analysis

Process: Processed data is stored in databases or other storage systems to ensure accessibility and data preservation.

Data Analysis

Definition: Examining and interpreting the processed data to identify patterns.

Process: Statistical analysis, machine learning, and data visualization are applied to conclusions and make informed decisions.

Data Output

Definition: Presenting the results of data analysis in a format.

Process: It involves generating reports, charts, graphs, or other visualizations.

Data Interpretation

Definition: Extracting meaning from the analysed data.

Process: Interpreting the results and making informed decisions based on the findings.

Data Storage (Archiving)

Definition: Archiving data for long-term storage and future reference.

Process: Archiving is essential for historical analysis and maintaining a record of past data.

Feedback

Definition: Receiving feedback on the processed data and its interpretation.

Process: Stakeholders provide feedback, that lead to adjustments in data processing methods or analysis.

There are various types of storage solutions, each serving different purposes and preferences. Here are examples of different kinds of storage, including online storage, external hard drives, and flash USB drives:

Online Storage or Cloud Storage

  • Their advantages are accessibility from any device with an internet connection; collaboration features for sharing and editing files with others and automatic synchronization for real-time updates.
  • Their considerations are dependence on internet connectivity and limited storage capacity for free plans, with options for additional paid storage.

As examples of Online Storage or Cloud Storage we have:

External Hard Drive

  • Their advantages are high storage capacity for large files and backups; no reliance on internet connectivity for data access and portable options for storage.
  • Their Considerations are vulnerable to physical damage as it is a physical device and has limited accessibility compared to cloud storage.

  • Their Considerations are limited storage capacity compared to external hard drives and possible to physical damage or loss as it has a small size.

Different parts of a company

You are visiting a company that works with IT to do a benchmarking with your company about how information is collected and stored in each sector. The name of this company is TechSolutions Inc. It operates in the technology sector and provides various IT solutions.

Here are the different parts of the company and the types of information they need for informed business decisions:

Click or tap to view content.

Management Department

Information Needed: Financial reports and forecasts for financial planning. Market trends and industry analyses to make strategic decisions. Employee performance data for effective workforce management.

How Information is Collected:

Financial reports and forecasts are collected through accounting software and financial management systems.

Market trends and industry analyses are collected through research, industry reports, and subscription services that provide data.

Employee performance data is often collected through performance reviews, project management tools, and employee feedback surveys.

How Information is Stored:

Financial data is stored in financial databases or enterprise resource planning (ERP) systems.

Market trends and industry analyses may be stored in a database for strategic planning.

Employee performance data can be stored in human resources management systems (HRMS).

Sales and Marketing Department

Information Needed: Customer data, including demographics and preferences, for targeted marketing campaigns. Sales performance metrics and conversion rates for evaluating marketing strategies. Competitor analysis to identify market opportunities and threats.

How Information is Collected:

Customer data is collected through customer relationship management (CRM) systems, online surveys, and social media interactions.

Sales performance metrics and conversion rates are tracked using sales management software and tools.

Competitor analysis involves monitoring competitors' activities, market research, and subscription services.

How Information is Stored:

Customer data is stored in a secure CRM database or marketing platform.

Sales performance metrics and conversion rates are stored in sales tools or integrated into CRM systems.

Competitor analysis data may be stored in a dedicated database or shared through collaborative platforms.

Product Development Department

Information Needed: Customer feedback and surveys for improving existing products and new ones. Technological advancements and innovations in the industry.

How Information is Collected:

Customer feedback and surveys are collected through feedback forms, product reviews, and customer interviews.

Technological advancements and innovations are identified through industry publications, tech forums, and partnerships.

How Information is Stored:

Customer feedback can be stored in product development databases or shared through tools.

Information on technological advancements may be stored in a knowledge base.

Customer Support Department

Information Needed: Customer service registers and feedback to address problems and improve service quality. Data on common customer queries and concerns for proactive support. Performance metrics to assess customer satisfaction levels.

How Information is Collected:

It is collected through customer support platforms, systems, and feedback forms.

Data are gathered through customer interactions and support databases.

Performance metrics for customer satisfaction are collected through surveys and feedback.

How Information is Stored:

Customer service registers and feedback data are stored in customer support databases.

Data on common customer queries may be stored in knowledge bases or support documentation systems.

Performance metrics for customer satisfaction are often stored in analytics tools or customer feedback platforms.

Human Resources Department

Information Needed: Employee performance data for evaluations and career development. Workforce demographics and diversity statistics. Training and development needs to improve employee skills.

How Information is Collected:

Employee performance data is collected through reviews, goal systems, and employee surveys.

Workforce demographics and diversity statistics are gathered through HR systems and employee databases.

Training and development needs are identified through performance assessments, skill gap analyses, and employee feedback.

How Information is Stored:

Employee performance data is typically stored in HRMS or performance systems.

Workforce demographics and diversity statistics may be stored in HR databases.

Training and development needs can be stored in learning management systems (LMS) or training databases.

IT Department

Information Needed: Data on network performance and security to ensure an IT infrastructure. Information on software and hardware updates to keep system efficiency. Cybersecurity threat intelligence to protect against risks.

How Information is Collected:

Data on network performance and security is collected through monitoring tools, security information and event management (SIEM) systems, and registers.

Information on software and hardware updates is collected through management systems and vendor communications.

Cybersecurity threat intelligence is collected through threat feeds, incident reports, and security research.

How Information is Stored:

Network performance and security data are stored in secure databases or SIEM systems.

Information on software and hardware updates may be stored in configuration management databases (CMDB) or documentation systems.

Cybersecurity threat intelligence can be stored in threat intelligence platforms or shared through secure communication channels.

Finance Department

Information Needed: Financial statements, including income statements and balance sheets. Cash flow reports for management. Tax updates for legal admission.

How Information is Collected:

Financial statements are generated through accounting software and audits.

Cash flow reports are through banking statements, and financial software.

Tax and regulatory compliance updates are obtained through legal consultations, tax software, and regulatory databases.

How Information is Stored:

Financial statements are typically stored in secure financial databases and ERP systems.

Cash flow reports may be stored in financial databases.

Tax and regulatory compliance updates can be stored in legal databases or compliance management systems.

Supply Chain and Logistics Department

Information Needed: Supplier performance data for vendor management. Inventory levels and demand forecasts for supply chain operations. Shipping and logistics data for product deliveries.

How Information is Collected:

Supplier performance data is collected through supplier evaluations and performance metrics.

Inventory levels and demand forecasts are obtained through inventory management systems, sales data, and demand planning tools.

Shipping and logistics data is gathered through shipping management systems, logistics providers, and tracking information.

How Information is Stored:

Supplier performance data can be stored in management systems or databases.

Inventory levels and demand forecasts may be stored in management systems or shared through collaboration platforms.

Shipping and logistics data is often stored in logistics databases or into supply chain management systems.

ODBC

ODBC, ou Open Database Connectivity, é um conjunto de classes-modelo para conexão disponível em qualquer linguagem de programação, diferindo-se da JDBC, que é exclusiva para Java.

JDBC

JDBC, ou Java EE Database Connectivity, é uma API (application programming interfaces) que possibilita a interação do código Java com um banco de dados, na qual as instruções SQL são executadas por meio de um driver, composto por um grupo de classes e interfaces desenvolvido em Java. Esse processo possibilita a execução de instruções SQLs que se conectarão com a base de dados, permitindo a manipulação dos valores.
