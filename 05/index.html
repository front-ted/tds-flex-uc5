Teacher: Good morning, class! Today, we're going to see the fascinating world of online business models. The digital world transformed how businesses operate with B2C, B2B, C2C, and m-commerce.

Let's start the explanation with B2C, or Business-to-Consumer commerce. This form is what we are familiar with. It involves transactions between businesses and individual consumers. Think of your favourite online shopping platform or the supermarket where you buy your product as a consumer. B2C is about delivering products and services that individual customers want or need.

Now, talking about B2B, or Business-to-Business commerce, businesses interact in transactions with other businesses. This could involve companies selling raw materials to other companies, or a software company providing its products to various companies. B2B transactions often involve larger quantities and more complex negotiations compared to B2C.

Next is C2C, or Consumer-to-Consumer commerce. Here, individual consumers interact in transactions with each other. Think of platforms where you can sell your items or exchange products with consumers. C2C transactions is the power of the community and the sharing economy.

Finally, let's explore the m-commerce, or Mobile Commerce. In this era of smartphones and mobile devices, commerce integrated with the digital world. M-commerce refers to transactions in mobile devices, as smartphones and tablets. Whether it's making purchases through mobile apps, mobile banking, or even mobile payments, the accessibility of m-commerce revolutionized how we do transactions.

In conclusion, these different forms of commerce - B2C, B2B, C2C, and m-commerce - illustrate the dynamic nature of our global economy. Embrace the opportunities presented by each, and remember, the future of commerce is not just about transactions; it's about building connections and promoting innovation in a world that is constantly changing. Thank you!

    ODBC

    ODBC, ou Open Database Connectivity, é um conjunto de classes-modelo para conexão disponível em qualquer linguagem de programação, diferindo-se da JDBC, que é exclusiva para Java.

    JDBC

    JDBC, ou Java EE Database Connectivity, é uma API (application programming interfaces) que possibilita a interação do código Java com um banco de dados, na qual as instruções SQL são executadas por meio de um driver, composto por um grupo de classes e interfaces desenvolvido em Java. Esse processo possibilita a execução de instruções SQLs que se conectarão com a base de dados, permitindo a manipulação dos valores.
