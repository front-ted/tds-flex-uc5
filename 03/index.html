William: Hello! I'm excited to see how the website is going on. Any progress updates?

Robert: Hi there! Yes, I've been working on the initial stages. Before we dive in, could you remind me of the purpose of your website?

William: Certainly! It's a portfolio website for my photography business. I want to showcase my work, attract potential clients, and provide a way for them to contact me for inquiries.

Robert: Great! So, the main characteristics you're looking for are a clean design to highlight your photography, contact forms for potential clients, and a user-friendly layout, correct?

William: Exactly. I want visitors to easily navigate through my portfolio and find the information they need without any hassle.

Robert: Got it. In terms of size, are you thinking of a multi-page website, or do you prefer a more compact, one-page design?

William: I'd like to keep it concise. Let's go for a one-page design that flows from one section to another.

Robert: Perfect. And regarding your target clients, are you focusing on a specific type, like individuals, events, or businesses?

William: Primarily individuals for portrait sessions and events, but I'd also like to attract small businesses interested in professional photography services.

Robert: Understood. I'll make sure to incorporate elements that appeal to both individuals and small businesses.

William: Thanks! Also, I'd love to be able to test the website before it goes live. Is that possible?

Robert: Absolutely. I'll set up a staging environment where you can preview the site and provide feedback. This way, we can make any necessary adjustments before the official launch.

William: Perfect! I appreciate that. When do you think I can take a look at the initial version?

Robert: I aim to have the first draft ready for your review by the end of next week. This will give us room for any revisions or additions you might want.

William: Sounds good. I'm looking forward to it. Let me know if you need anything from my end in the meantime.

Robert: Will do! If you think of any specific details you'd like to add, feel free to share them. I want to make sure the website reflects your vision.

William: Thanks for being so attentive. I appreciate your hard work on this.

Robert: My pleasure! I'll be in touch soon with the preview. If you have any questions in the meantime, don't hesitate to reach out.

William: Will do. Thanks again!

ODBC

ODBC, ou Open Database Connectivity, é um conjunto de classes-modelo para conexão disponível em qualquer linguagem de programação, diferindo-se da JDBC, que é exclusiva para Java.

JDBC

JDBC, ou Java EE Database Connectivity, é uma API (application programming interfaces) que possibilita a interação do código Java com um banco de dados, na qual as instruções SQL são executadas por meio de um driver, composto por um grupo de classes e interfaces desenvolvido em Java. Esse processo possibilita a execução de instruções SQLs que se conectarão com a base de dados, permitindo a manipulação dos valores.
